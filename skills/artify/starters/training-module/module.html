Lesson setup

Start with the learning outcome.

This starter is for material that should read like a guide, present like a workshop deck, and still give the learner small feedback loops instead of dead static text.

By the end

Learners can explain what hybrid starters are and when to choose them over a standard long-read or deck.

Format

One file, four panels, reveals for quizzes, appendix for instructor backup.

Presenter note: say the outcome before the content so people know what to watch for.
Core concepts

Teach with small chunks.

Read mode

Best when the learner wants context and pace control.

Focus mode

Best for facilitation, demos, and screen-sharing one chapter at a time.

Slides mode

Best for presenter-led delivery with keyboard navigation.

Appendix blocks

Best for advanced material that should not overwhelm the first pass.

Checkpoint

Let the reader test themselves.

Question 1 - When is a hybrid starter better than a plain deck?

When the artifact also needs to survive as a readable long-form reference after the meeting.

Question 2 - Why hide appendix content behind an audience toggle?

To keep the primary story clean while preserving evidence and backup material in the same file.

Wrap-up

End with what to do next.

Action

Duplicate the starter, replace the lesson blocks, and keep at least one checkpoint section.
