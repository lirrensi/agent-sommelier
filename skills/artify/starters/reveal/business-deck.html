Revenue hit 4.2M, up 23% year over year. Retention held at 89%, which is above our 85% target. All three metrics are green.

Revenue by Region

APAC drove most of the growth — up 40% from Q3. EMEA was flat. North America grew modestly at 8%. The APAC spike is from the new enterprise deals we closed in October.

What's Driving Growth

  • Enterprise deals in APAC closed 2x faster than forecast
  • Self-serve onboarding reduced CAC by 18%
  • Product-led growth: 34% of new signups from referrals
Three things are working. The APAC enterprise motion is ahead of plan. Self-serve onboarding is paying off — CAC dropped 18%. And referrals are now a third of new signups, which means the product is selling itself.

Two Risks to Watch

  • EMEA churn: 3 key accounts at risk of non-renewal in Q1
  • Infrastructure cost: cloud spend up 31% — outpacing revenue growth
Two red flags. EMEA has three accounts up for renewal that are showing disengagement signals. And our cloud infrastructure costs are growing faster than revenue — 31% vs 23%. We need to address both in Q1 planning.

Q1 Priorities

  • Launch EMEA retention program (owner: Sarah)
  • Infrastructure cost audit (owner: DevOps)
  • Scale APAC enterprise motion to Japan market
Three priorities for Q1. Sarah owns the EMEA retention push. DevOps is doing a cost audit — they'll report findings by end of January. And we're extending the APAC playbook to Japan.
