Policy snapshot

Open with the rule, the reason, and who it applies to.

This starter is for policies that should be readable like a handbook but presentable like a rollout briefing. The role filter makes it easy to show only what matters to a given audience.

Rule

All external-facing launches require a documented owner, rollback path, and stakeholder notification plan.

Reason

Without a clear owner and rollback path, launch work turns into finger-pointing theater the moment something breaks.

Presenter note: say what changed, why now, and who must care. Nobody wants to decode policy poetry.
Role-specific guidance

Filter the playbook by responsibility.

Managers

Confirm the launch owner, sign off on customer-impact messaging, and escalate unresolved dependencies.

Operators

Validate runbooks, monitoring, and rollback commands before the launch window opens.

Contractors

Route all external communications and credential changes through the designated internal owner.

Examples and exceptions

Make the rule feel usable, not abstract.

Good example

Feature launch with one owner, pre-written customer notes, observability checks, and a rollback command pasted into the page.

Bad example

"We will figure it out live" followed by panic and three Slack threads at once.

Required actions

End with an operational checklist.

Before launch

Assign owner, approve comms, attach runbook.

During launch

Track status live, update stakeholders, log deviations.

After launch

Record outcome, unresolved issues, and policy exceptions.
