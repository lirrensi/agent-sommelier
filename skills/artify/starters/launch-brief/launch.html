Launch Brief

For rollout planning that should be readable before launch day and presentable during review.

1/4
Launch flow
Goal

Start with what the launch is trying to achieve.

This starter helps a launch plan read like a story, present like a review, and survive as the single reference after kickoff.

Launch objective

Introduce the hybrid starter family with enough examples that users instantly get the value.

Success signal

Users can identify a hybrid starter use case without reading a giant explanation wall.

Presenter note: say what success looks like in one sentence. Launches rot when nobody shares the same target.
Audience and message

Who is this for and what should they understand?

Primary audience

People who want documents that can also perform as presentations.

Message

A modern artifact can be readable, presentable, and adaptable without being rebuilt from scratch.

Plan

Sequence the rollout.

Phase 1

Publish the core family and update the skill docs.

Phase 2

Demo the best starters and gather which ones people actually copy first.

Phase 3

Refine naming, defaults, and richer interactions based on usage.

Readiness

Show what must be true before shipping.

Must have

Readable starter files, documented catalog entries, and verified paths.

Watch-outs

Inconsistent quality and weak discoverability if the docs lag behind the files.

Appendix

Use for launch FAQ, stakeholder mapping, rollback plan, or support notes.
